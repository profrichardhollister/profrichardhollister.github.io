Courses¶

Current Courses¶

MTH 337 - Intro to Scientific and Mathematical Computing website

This is a project based course that introduces mathematics students to the tools and techniques used in scientific and mathematical computing. The course uses Python as a platform and focuses on about 8 independent projects over the semester. Students are encouraged to work in groups, but each student must turn in their own original project report as a Jupyter notebook.

MTH 309 - Intro to Linear Algebra syllabus

This is a standard introductory course in linear algebra with an emphasis on active learning and fostering student success. To get students to actively engage with the material, I created activities for each topic to be completed by students in groups during class. This semester also saw a switch from the typical exam format (2 midterms and a final) to a proficiency format, the details of which can be found in the syllabus.

Previous Courses¶

MTH 420 - Advanced Linear Algebra syllabus

This course is meant to be a second course in linear algbrea for undergraduates and focuses on theory proof. Advanced topics include matrix factorizations, equivalence relations and canonical representations, quadratic forms and positive (semi-) definite matrices, and select topics from numerical linear algebra.

MTH 309 - Intro to Linear Algebra syllabus

This is a your standard introductory course on linear algebra. Topics ranged from systems of equations and matrix algebra to vector spaces and linear transformations to orthogonal projections and least squares.

MTH 122 - Survey of Calculus and Its Applications II syllabus

The second semester in a two semester calculus sequence for life sciences and non-STEM majors. The focus of the course is on the applications of calculus to real world problems along with the computational tools and techniques needed to solve those problems. Topics included optimization and lagrange multipliers, integration techniques and work problems, and numerical solutions to differential equations.
